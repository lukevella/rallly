Poll admin — the page that turns a poll into a plan

Poll management moves out of the session-identified /poll/{id} page into a dedicated, login-required page in the dashboard shell. The organizer's job: get people in, chase the stragglers, watch results converge, finalize a time — without leaving Rallly. Wireframes are structure and flow only; no visual design decisions are being made here.

Rev 2 changes: results redesigned around a ranked option list that scales to 40+ options (the participants × options grid is gone as the primary view) · a persistent participation cockpit puts invite and remind front and center · people merged into one participants table · manual "add participant" removed — participants join through the invite page like everyone else.

Reading key Grayscale = the wireframe Blue = annotations, not UI Amber = Pro gate moment Green dot = responded status ▨ Dashed = reserved for a later project
01

Page anatomy

The page is organized around the organizer's loop: participation first (a persistent cockpit with response progress, invite, and remind), then results, then the people themselves. Everything that scales with poll size — options, participants — is a vertical list that can search, group, and paginate. The rail holds context and future growth, never primary workflow.

Annotations

  1. 1

    Header: breadcrumb back to Polls, poll title, status chip (Live / Paused / Finalized), Manage menu. Invite lives in the cockpit, not the header — one home, maximum prominence.

  2. 2

    The cockpit is the thesis of the page: the full flow — invite, chase, converge — is managed from here. It never scrolls away conceptually: on long pages it can stick below the page header.

  3. 3

    Vertical everything: options as rows means 4 options and 44 behave the same — the list just gets longer, with grouping and "show all". No axis explodes horizontally.

  4. 4

    One door for participants: people enter via the invite page (shared link or personal email link). The admin manages responses; it doesn't create them. This kills the old confusion of the participant table being misused as invites.

Annotations

  1. 1

    The cockpit reports the number that matters: invitee response rate, with link responders counted separately (they can't be chased individually — no email). Progress bar tracks invitees.

  2. 2

    Send reminder is a first-class action, not buried in a menu: one click opens a confirm listing the 5 pending invitees (deselect individuals, optional personal note). Per-person remind also lives on each pending row below. Guardrail: one reminder per invitee per 24h.

  3. 3

    Sort covers both mental models: "most votes" (default) for deciding, "date" for scanning the calendar shape. Search is unnecessary here — sorting does the work at 44 options.

  4. 4

    Rank 1 is the only row with a Finalize button. Any other row can be finalized from its expanded view — deliberate friction: choosing a non-leader should be a considered act.

  5. 5

    Row expansion replaces the grid's cross-reference job: "who can make this time" appears under the option, grouped by vote. The per-person full ballot lives on the participant row (note 8) — the two axes of the old grid, each queried from its own list.

  6. 6

    Top 5 by default, rest on demand. With 44 options the tail is noise until asked for; "show all" renders the full ranked list (virtualized).

  7. 7

    One people table, three states: Responded (invited, has ballot), Via link (ballot, no invite), Pending (invite, no ballot). Roster and participant list are the same thing here — no duplicated concepts. And no "add participant": the admin manages responses, it doesn't create them.

  8. 8

    Row overflow: View votes (their full ballot in a side panel — the vertical slice of the old grid), Edit votes, Remove. Pending rows instead get Remind · Copy personal link · Revoke invite.

  9. 9

    Options grouped by month with live vote counts. A 0 ✓ row is a pruning candidate the organizer can spot and remove; removing an option with votes warns before deleting them. ＋ opens the calendar picker.

Annotations

  1. 1

    Sorted by score, pre-selected to the leader, filterable. At 44 options the filter (by date text, weekday, month) earns its place here even though the page list doesn't need it — this is a commitment moment, not a scan.

  2. 2

    Pressure release, not a blocker: pending invitees are surfaced with a shortcut to remind — closing the loop between chasing and deciding. The organizer chooses to wait or proceed.

  3. 3

    Consequence preview: who's in, who's out for the chosen slot, before committing. No surprises after the emails go out.

  4. 4

    Notify is a choice, checked by default. Recurring organizers sometimes finalize retroactively and don't want a mail blast.

    Finalized: the event replaces the cockpit and results as the hero. Links out to the existing event page; the poll page remains the record of how the time was chosen.

Annotations

  1. 1

    The gate shows the feature doing its job: a grayed roster preview with a Remind button — invite, track, chase as one capability. Reminders don't need their own gate: they only exist where invitees exist, so the invite gate covers them.

  2. 2

    Duplicate keeps its existing gate: badge in the menu, paywall on click. Prime for recurring organizers — the exact Pro audience.

09

Built for what's next — without designing it

The rail is an append-only stack of context cards; the participants table establishes the row contract (avatar · identity · status chip · action) that the collaborators card will reuse for its own pending-invite rows. Nothing in the main column moves when collaborators or activity arrive.

Details

Collaborators (Pro)

Same row anatomy as the participants table: avatar · name · Pending/Active chip · overflow (resend, revoke). Poll-level, capped per poll, occupies no seat.

Activity

Timeline card, read-only. Lowest priority position; can paginate internally.

Also reserved, off-canvas:

"Shared with me" polls land in the collaborator's own dashboard poll list — no layout impact here.

Private polls change the invite hero's composition (email form primary, no share link) — a swap inside the cockpit, not a page change.

The status chip in the page header has room for a visibility label (Public / Private) later.

Why lists, not tabs or grids

  1. →

    "Who hasn't answered" and "how are results converging" are the same glance — tabs would split them. And every scalable surface on this page (results, participants, options) is a vertical list, so growth in any dimension is more scroll, never more width and never a redesign.

10

Open questions before hi-fi

  1. Is finalize free or Pro? Today "Schedule" triggers the paywall for free users, but the brief lists finalizing under core lifecycle and the conversion bet has moved to invites. Recommend free — finalize is the job to be done; gating it competes with the invite gate for the same moment of intent.
  2. Reminder guardrails. Wireframed: one reminder per invitee per 24h, per-person and bulk. Do we also want an automatic nudge (e.g. auto-remind pending invitees after N days) as a setting, or is manual-only right for the MVP? Recommend manual-only first; auto-remind is a natural Pro follow-up.
  3. Does a compact grid survive anywhere? The ranked list is the only results view in these wireframes. For ≤ ~8 options and ≤ ~10 participants the old participants × options grid is genuinely pleasant. Ship list-only first, or keep a "grid" toggle that auto-hides at scale? Recommend list-only first; add the toggle later if small-poll users miss it.
  4. Can a finalized poll be un-finalized? The wireframes treat it as terminal (delete the event to undo). If reopening must exist, it belongs in the Manage menu with a strong confirm.
  5. Notify participants when options change? Adding/removing options on a live poll may deserve an optional "let participants know" nudge — possibly reusing the reminder machinery. Needs a product call.
  6. Roster matching edge case: an invitee who responds via the shared link under a different email shows as Pending forever (and keeps receiving reminders). A "link to participant" action on the pending row could resolve it — deferred to the invites implementation spec.
  7. Where does the legacy /poll/{id} URL go? Owners: redirect here. Guests who managed via session: the Unified Invite project's account prompt is the on-ramp — needs a decided redirect + claim flow before this page ships.
